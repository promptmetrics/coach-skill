# Client Ledger

A living record of what was sold, what was delivered with proof, and what was not delivered and why. It turns an emotionally charged conversation into a fact-based one.

## How to use it

- Build it BEFORE the next hard conversation, not during it. Drafting it under fire defeats the point.
- Keep it factual and dated. Every row is a checkable fact or it doesn't belong here.
- It may make an unreasonable person angrier, because they lose the ambiguity they were hiding in. That is still a win: the facts go on record, and your credibility with every reasonable person in the room goes up even if the unreasonable one doesn't budge.

| 2026-07-14 | Weekly enablement session for sales team | Not delivered | — | Client deferred access to sandbox; rescheduled to 2026-07-21 |

## How to use it in the conversation

When the client re-litigates an emotional claim ("you never delivered X," "we agreed Y"), do not re-argue the feeling. Point to the ledger: the date, the item, the status, the proof. This is the Respond beat of LAER. The facts do the work so you don't have to.

# the-human-layer

> Coaches you through a real soft-skills conversation — feedback, pushback, a hard client or team talk. Use it to rehearse or prepare for a difficult workplace conversation.

## What it does

Instead of handing out generic advice, it runs a structured five-step method — **Ground it, Name the pattern, Roleplay it, Debrief with teeth, Log it** — on whatever real workplace conversation you bring: a report who's underperforming, a client pushing scope, a peer you need to confront, or a talk you've been avoiding. It roleplays the counterpart, gives you the actual words to use, debriefs honestly, and then logs the session as a Growth Log entry so the coaching becomes a tracked practice rather than a one-off.

The method blends three frameworks: **LAER** (Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond) gives the conversation its skeleton; ***Never Split the Difference*** sharpens the language inside it (mirroring, labeling, calibrated questions); ***The Art of War*** shapes how the rehearsal counterpart behaves, so the practice is hard enough to be worth something. Each session you choose LAER alone or LAER with the Never Split the Difference layer, and optionally opt into the Art of War layer to make the counterpart tougher and less predictable.

The method comes from an operations leader trained as an anthropologist, who ran weekly soft-skills coaching for her team for years and ended up in tech almost by accident. What she found there left a sour taste: in a results-driven industry, the human being at the center of every deal, every project, and every client relationship quietly becomes a commodity. **The Human Layer** is her response — the belief that soft skills are not the soft part of the job, they are the actual job, underneath the tech, the tools, and the contracts. Every session serves one outcome: you leave able to be seen, felt, and heard as an equal at any table. Full method detail lives in `SKILL.md` and `references/the-method.md`.

## When it activates

- "Help me coach my team on feedback"
- "I need to talk to my report about X"
- "How do I handle a difficult conversation with a client"
- "Roleplay this conversation with me"
- Any mention of coaching, difficult conversations, giving feedback, handling pushback, or preparing for a hard conversation at work
- When a manager describes a team or client situation and seems to want help thinking through how to approach it — even without the word "coach"

## The five steps

1. **Ground it** — get the real, specific situation before doing anything else.
2. **Name the pattern** — identify the dynamic in two or three sentences and give it a name. Then choose your framework: **LAER alone**, or **LAER + *Never Split the Difference*** (mirroring, labeling, calibrated questions). Optionally opt into the ***Art of War* layer** to make the counterpart tougher and less predictable.
3. **Roleplay it** — play the counterpart, make it genuinely hard, offer sound bites that match the framework you chose.
4. **Debrief with teeth** — ask how you think you did, give an honest read, rerun the moment that needs work to test whether you applied the feedback, close with three tips and a sound bite.
5. **Log it** — turn the session into a Growth Log entry (a Sheet tab when Drive is connected, otherwise a downloadable markdown file) recording the framework used, the Art of War layer, the coachability check, and the sound bites used, so the coaching becomes a tracked practice.
